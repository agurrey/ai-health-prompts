# AI Health Prompts — 5-Module System

Free, open-source system prompts for AI-assisted health coaching. Drop any of them into ChatGPT, Claude, or DeepSeek.

Built by a personal trainer with 5+ years of experience in functional movement and injury rehabilitation. Every prompt is backed by real coaching methodology, not generic AI output.

## The Modules

| Module | File | What it does |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Spinal Health** | [sedentary_spinal_health.md](sedentary_spinal_health.md) | Back pain & mobility protocol for desk workers. McGill Big 3, movement snacks, ergonomics, pain education. Phase 0-3 progression. |
| **CrossFit Programming** | [crossfit_triple_block.md](crossfit_triple_block.md) | Hybrid Oly + Strength + Conditioning programming. 4-7 week cycles, autoregulation, injury decision trees. |
| **Dumbbell Home Training** | [dumbbell_home_training.md](dumbbell_home_training.md) | Full-body training with 2 pairs of dumbbells. 5 movement patterns, 7-step progression ladder, Phase 1-4. |
| **Whole Food Nutrition** | [whole_food_nutrition.md](whole_food_nutrition.md) | Zero ultra-processed eating framework. NOVA classification, plate model, transition system, budget strategies. |
| **Sleep Optimization** | [sleep_optimization.md](sleep_optimization.md) | Evidence-based sleep protocol. Circadian rhythm, environment, breathing techniques, Phase 1-4 implementation. |

## How to Use

1. Copy the full content of any `.md` file
2. Paste it as a system prompt (or first message) in your AI of choice
3. Start chatting — the AI will guide you through an intake and generate your protocol

Each module works standalone, but they cross-reference each other when relevant (e.g., sleep affects pain sensitivity, training requires nutrition support).

## Who Is This For

- **Desk workers** with back pain, stiffness, or zero exercise routine
- **Home trainers** with minimal equipment who want real programming
- **Anyone** who wants to eat better without counting calories
- **People** who know they sleep badly but don't know where to start
- **CrossFit athletes** who want intelligent periodized programming

## Design Principles

- **Progressive** — phases with gates, not arbitrary timelines
- **Bilingual** — English and Spanish (exercise names always in English)
- **Evidence-based** — McGill, Cook, Walker, Fogg, NOVA classification
- **Safe** — injury decision trees, red flags, medical disclaimers, referral triggers
- **Adherent** — habit design (Tiny Habits), environment setup, the 2-minute rule
- **Honest** — no "toxic" food talk, no fear-based spine language, no supplement-first approach

## Knowledge Sources

These prompts distill methodology from:
- Stuart McGill — *Low Back Disorders* (spine stability, Big 3)
- Gray Cook — *Movement* (functional screening, corrective hierarchy)
- Thomas Myers — *Anatomy Trains* (fascial integration)
- Matthew Walker — *Why We Sleep* (sleep architecture, circadian science)
- James Nestor — *Breath* (nasal breathing, sleep breathing)
- BJ Fogg — *Tiny Habits* (behavior design, anchoring)
- James Clear — *Atomic Habits* (environment design, habit stacking)
- Kelly Starrett — *Becoming a Supple Leopard* (mobility, movement quality)
- NOVA Classification — ultra-processed food framework
- NASM — corrective exercise methodology

## Disclaimer

These prompts generate **general health guidance, not medical advice**. They include built-in disclaimers and referral triggers for conditions that require professional supervision. If you have a diagnosed medical condition, always consult your healthcare provider.
